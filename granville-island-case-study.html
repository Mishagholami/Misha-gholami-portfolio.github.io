Project Overview & User Research

Granville Island is a vibrant cultural and public space in Vancouver. Its existing website, however, presented challenges for visitors seeking information, leading to a less-than-optimal digital experience. Our research aimed to understand user behaviors and pain points.

Research Methods

  • **Heuristic Evaluation:** Identified usability issues on the current website.
  • **User Interviews (N=15):** Engaged with frequent visitors and potential tourists to understand their needs and frustrations.
  • **Card Sorting:** Explored optimal information architecture for content organization.
  • **Competitor Analysis:** Studied other major tourist attraction websites for best practices.

Key User Insights & Personas

Our research revealed common themes and allowed us to develop representative user personas:

Navigation Confusion

Users struggled to find specific vendors, events, or parking information quickly.

"I just want to know where the public market is and if there's parking nearby, but it's buried."

Event Discovery Gap

Many were unaware of the diverse events and activities happening on the island.

"I had no idea there was a jazz festival happening until I got there by chance."

Visual Underrepresentation

The website didn't fully convey the vibrant and unique atmosphere of Granville Island.

"The website doesn't do justice to how lively and colorful the island actually is."

Persona: The Local Explorer

A Vancouver resident looking for new experiences, specific vendors, or local events. Values quick access to information and up-to-date listings.

Persona: The Tourist Planner

A visitor planning their trip, needing comprehensive information on attractions, transportation, and dining options. Values clear maps and itineraries.

UX Strategy & Design Principles

Our strategy focused on clarity, discoverability, and visual appeal:

Intuitive Navigation

Simplify the site structure and navigation paths to help users find information effortlessly.

  • Clear main menu
  • Prominent search

Enhanced Discoverability

Promote events, workshops, and hidden gems more effectively.

  • Featured events section
  • Interactive map

Vibrant Visuals

Capture the unique atmosphere of Granville Island through rich imagery and engaging design.

  • High-quality photography
  • Dynamic layouts

Design Solutions & Before/After

We implemented several key design changes to address the identified issues:

New Homepage Layout

A more inviting and organized homepage with clear calls to action for popular sections like the Public Market and Events.

Interactive Map

An interactive map allowing users to filter by vendor type, locate amenities, and get directions.

Event Calendar

A comprehensive and filterable event calendar, making it easy to discover what's happening.

Before & After Comparison

A visual comparison highlighting the transformation of key pages.

Old Homepage

Cluttered layout, less engaging visuals, and difficult navigation.

New Homepage

Clean, modern, and visually appealing with improved information hierarchy.

Project Impact & Reflection

The redesign is expected to significantly improve the digital experience for Granville Island visitors.

+50%

Projected increase in event attendance due to better discoverability.

Reduced

Visitor frustration with finding information and navigating the site.

Enhanced

Brand perception, reflecting Granville Island's vibrant character.

This project underscored the importance of deep user research in transforming a functional but unengaging website into a delightful and informative digital gateway. The challenge was to balance the rich, eclectic nature of Granville Island with a clear, intuitive user experience. The iterative design process, from card sorting to high-fidelity mockups, was crucial in achieving this balance.
